# HelpATL Activist Organizations Research 001

- Date: 2026-03-10
- Portal: `helpatl`
- Scope: assess ATL DSA and similar metro-Atlanta activist / issue-organizing organizations for HelpATL inclusion
- Decision: `ATL DSA = onboard now`; build next wave from `source-ready` activist orgs with clear public event or get-involved surfaces

## Why this matters

HelpATL is already credible on volunteer discovery and public-process participation.
The remaining gap is issue-based civic action: organizations that help people organize,
train, canvass, volunteer, and join campaigns outside purely official government channels.

## ATL DSA

- Organization: Atlanta Democratic Socialists of America
- Site: `https://atldsa.org/`
- Events surface: `https://atldsa.org/events/`
- Join surface: `https://atldsa.org/join`
- Read: `source-ready`

Why it fits:

- public events page is live and stable
- page exposes multiple public Google Calendar embeds for chapter-wide and branch-level activity
- events are exactly the kind of issue-based civic participation HelpATL is still thin on:
  - organizing meetings
  - trainings / political education
  - issue-campaign actions
  - canvassing / outreach

Recommended shape:

- `event` source for calendar-driven public activity
- optional later `structured opportunity` for chapter joining / organizer pathways

## Integration rules

For activist organizations, HelpATL should keep one hard boundary:

1. label them as `civic action`, `advocacy`, `organizing`, or issue-based participation
2. do not mix them into `Government Meetings` or official neutral public-process lanes
3. keep official/public-interest sources and advocacy/member organizations clearly attributable
