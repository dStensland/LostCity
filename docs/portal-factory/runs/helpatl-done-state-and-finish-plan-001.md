## 1) What `done` means

HelpATL is `done` when it is the best Atlanta portal for:

1. `Do something this week`
2. `Commit to a cause`
3. `Track and join civic process`
4. `Stay smart on policy and public decisions`
5. `Find help, not just ways to help`

That does not mean infinite expansion. It means the current product surfaces are
authoritative, balanced, and trustworthy enough that the next work becomes
maintenance and selective refinement instead of platform buildout.

## 2) Product-level done state

HelpATL can be considered product-complete when all of these are true:

### A. Immediate action is strong

- `Volunteer This Week` is deep, trustworthy, and not visibly broken
- the top volunteer experience stays above a meaningful density bar
- major volunteer sources do not show blank, generic, stale, or broken listings

### B. Ongoing participation is credible

- `Ongoing Opportunities` and `Commit to a Cause` are broad enough to serve real users
- ongoing roles are source-linked and attributable
- civic, humanitarian, family-support, refugee, legal-aid, and public-health roles are all represented

### C. Civic process authority is real

- neighborhood, school-board, city/county, and statewide democracy participation are all present
- HelpATL can point users to official or high-trust process surfaces, not just activist/event proxies
- the policy-nerd audience can track process, not just advocacy

### D. Policy/news feels intentional

- HelpATL’s local policy/news mix is more serious than Atlanta’s parent mix
- reporting, explainers, and process coverage work together
- the policy layer feels like a feature, not an accident of inheritance

### E. Support resources are complete enough

- all six support-directory lanes are credible
- the weakest lane is no longer obviously thin
- the directory is trustworthy even without real-time availability

## 3) Measurable exit criteria

HelpATL reaches `done` when all of these are satisfied at the same time:

### Events / civic / volunteer

- live event sources remain stable at `30+` high-trust sources
- active channels remain coherent at roughly the current scope, not inflated
- `Volunteer This Week` remains strong with:
  - `25+` distinct next-7-day high-quality volunteer opportunities on the main experience
  - `0` blank descriptions across the approved major volunteer-source set in the next 30 days
  - no known high-visibility broken URLs in the audited top sample

### Ongoing opportunities

- active ongoing opportunities: `60+`
- source-null ongoing opportunities: `0`
- `Commit to a Cause` and `Ongoing Opportunities` both remain materially populated

### Policy / civic authority

- HelpATL local policy/news sources: `4+`
- HelpATL resolved policy/news posts in 30 days: `175+`
- `Georgia Democracy Watch` becomes a real lane:
  - `5+` useful next-30-day items
  - sourced from stable official or clearly high-trust process surfaces

### Support directory

- every support lane is at `20+` organizations or clearly justified as smaller
- `Work, Money & Daily Life` stays at `20+`

### Governance / trust

- manifest matches live HelpATL source state
- `Live Event Sources` and `Ongoing Opportunity Sources` remain clearly separated in tooling/docs
- no unresolved federation-critical migration drift for HelpATL-specific work

## 4) What is already effectively done

These are no longer the main risks:

- HelpATL has a real civic + humanitarian shape
- ongoing-opportunity traceability is closed
- policy/news merge model is live
- `Work, Money & Daily Life` hit its current target
- major volunteer-source blank descriptions are closed
- Atlanta and HelpATL now have meaningfully different news tone

## 5) What is not done yet

There are three meaningful gaps left between current state and finish state:

### Gap 1. Statewide process authority

This is the largest remaining strategic gap.

Current state:

- `Georgia Democracy Watch` is still too thin
- official statewide process coverage is not yet strong enough for the policy-nerd user

Done state:

- a stable official or high-trust process stack for:
  - State Election Board
  - major election administration dates
  - at least one additional official statewide process surface

### Gap 2. Volunteer concentration

Current state:

- volunteer supply is still heavily concentrated in a few major sources

Done state:

- concentration is understood and honestly managed
- top experience remains strong even if concentration does not materially improve
- no quality regressions from the dominant sources

### Gap 3. Policy-watch authority depth

Current state:

- HelpATL now has strong reporting/explainer sources
- but the local policy spine is still only one layer short of “home base” quality

Done state:

- reporting + explainers + official process feel like one coherent feature set

## 6) Finish workstreams

The remaining path should be run as four finish workstreams.

### Workstream 1. Close statewide process authority

Goal:

- make `Georgia Democracy Watch` a real destination

Tasks:

1. Identify a stable official alternative to the access-blocked statewide source surface
2. Add `1-2` official or clearly high-trust statewide process sources
3. Route those items into `Georgia Democracy Watch`
4. Verify the lane reaches `5+` useful next-30-day items

Stop condition:

- if the only viable official sources still require brittle scraping, stop and formalize the fallback authority model instead of hacking around it

### Workstream 2. Deepen the policy spine

Goal:

- make HelpATL feel like the Atlanta civic-policy home base

Tasks:

1. Keep the current local policy source set stable:
   - `atlanta-civic-circle`
   - `georgia-recorder`
   - `capitol-beat`
   - `gbpi`
2. Verify the policy layer is prominent and coherent in the feed
3. Decide whether one more high-signal policy analysis source is necessary, or whether the current set is sufficient once statewide process is solved

Exit:

- users can follow reporting, analysis, and official process in one place without the layer feeling thin

### Workstream 3. Lock volunteer quality

Goal:

- keep the volunteer lane trustworthy even if source concentration remains

Tasks:

1. Continue upstream hardening on the dominant sources only when a real quality defect appears
2. Maintain the major-source quality audit:
   - blank descriptions
   - broken URLs
   - stale rows
   - generic titles
3. Do not burn time trying to force weak breadth sources into “balancing” roles they do not actually play

Exit:

- volunteer quality remains high under current source concentration

### Workstream 4. Close the support/resource finish line

Goal:

- leave the support directory in a stable, credible, maintainable state

Tasks:

1. Verify the current six sections still read cleanly
2. Fill any remaining obvious thin spots only if they are truly user-facing gaps
3. Avoid turning the directory into a generic undifferentiated list

Exit:

- all lanes feel intentional and none feel abandoned

## 7) Recommended execution order

1. `Statewide process authority`
2. `Policy spine depth`
3. `Volunteer quality lock`
4. `Support-directory finish pass`

Reason:

- the biggest remaining strategic gap is authority, not content volume
- once authority is solved, the remaining work is mostly hardening and closure

## 8) Operational definition of done

HelpATL is ready to move from `build mode` to `operate mode` when:

1. the statewide process gap is closed or intentionally resolved with a durable fallback
2. the policy/news layer feels like a first-class feature
3. the volunteer lane is high-trust and stable
4. the support directory no longer has an obviously weak lane
5. no major governance-quality gaps remain in source linkage, federation, or portal contracts

At that point, new work should default to:

- periodic audits
- selective source additions
- quality maintenance

not another broad product-expansion cycle.
