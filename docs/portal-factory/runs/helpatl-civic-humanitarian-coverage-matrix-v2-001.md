## What Is Actually Strong Now

HelpATL is now clearly strong in:

- `Neighborhood participation`
- `Government-watch basics`
- `Food security`

Those are not hypothetical strengths anymore. They show up in live volume and in the portal structure.

## What Has Moved From Thin To Credible

These were weak earlier and are now good enough to claim:

- `civic_engagement`
- `immigrant_refugee`
- `family_support`
- `legal_aid`
- `health_wellness`
- `survivor support`

This is the biggest strategic change in the current HelpATL build. The portal is
no longer just a drop-in volunteer feed with some civic edges. It now has a real
participation layer and a real humanitarian breadth layer.

## What Is Still Not Definitive

HelpATL is not yet the definitive civic-humanitarian source because three things
are still incomplete:

1. `Official clerk-managed boards/commissions notices`
   The live official Atlanta clerk/public-notice path is still blocked by `403`.

2. `Support-resource directory layer`
   HelpATL is strong at actions and opportunities, weaker at mapping support systems
   and ‚Äúwhere help exists.‚Äù

3. `Metro depth consistency`
   Metro breadth is improving, but some lanes are still much deeper in Atlanta/Fulton
   than in Cobb/Gwinnett/DeKalb.

## Recommendation

Do not keep expanding sideways indefinitely.

The right next steps are:

1. `Lock and audit`
   Re-check freshness, duplication, and broken signup paths.

2. `Solve the official clerk/public-notice blocker`
   This is the cleanest remaining civic authority gap.

3. `Start the support-resource layer`
   That is the next move that actually changes HelpATL from ‚Äústrong participation portal‚Äù
   into ‚Äúdefinitive civic and humanitarian source.‚Äù
