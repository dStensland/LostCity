# HelpATL Volunteer Breadth Audit

Date: 2026-03-09

## Mission Check

Original mission:

- make HelpATL excellent at metro Atlanta volunteer discovery
- bias toward drop-in opportunities
- still represent longer-term commitments
- cover real breadth across humanitarian and civic involvement

## Current State

HelpATL is already strong in:

- food access
- environment
- housing/service builds
- mentoring and youth education
- core city/county/school-board meetings

It is weak in:

- health / medical humanitarian volunteering
- animal welfare volunteering
- refugee / immigrant support
- homelessness system / social-service volunteering
- legal-aid and rights-oriented community service
- civic action beyond government meetings

## Evidence

- next-30-day volunteer inventory is still dominated by `hands-on-atlanta`
- structured volunteer opportunities only cover:
  - `environment`
  - `food_security`
  - `youth_education`
  - `education`
  - `family_support`
- the repo already has existing source support for:
  - `medshare`
  - `atlanta-humane-society`
  - `lifeline-animal-project`
  - `hope-atlanta`
  - `irc-atlanta`
  - `atlanta-legal-aid`
- of those, the currently active high-confidence additions are:
  - `medshare`
  - `atlanta-humane-society`
  - `lifeline-animal-project`
  - `marta-board`

## Action Taken

Updated the HelpATL volunteer manifest to add already-active breadth sources:

- `medshare`
- `atlanta-humane-society`
- `lifeline-animal-project`
- `marta-board`

Applied them in the least-complex way:

- broader source subscriptions
- broader `Volunteer This Week`
- broader `Ongoing Opportunities`
- clarified `Government Meetings` copy to include transit boards

## Why This Is The Right Move

This improves breadth without changing product shape.

Users still get the same simple experience:

- volunteer this week
- find longer-term causes
- follow civic action

But HelpATL now moves closer to a truthful “humanitarian + civic” claim instead of mainly “food/environment/meetings”.

## Remaining Gaps

Still not solved:

- `hope-atlanta` is inactive
- `irc-atlanta` is inactive
- `atlanta-legal-aid` is inactive

Those are the next highest-leverage humanitarian additions once validated, because they would add:

- homelessness and shelter-system support
- refugee and immigrant support
- rights, clinic, and legal-aid involvement
