## 7) Assessment

### Strong

1. Volunteer depth is real and well above minimum viability.
2. Ongoing opportunities are broad across civic, humanitarian, legal, housing, family, and health.
3. Neighborhood and civic action lanes are no longer token.
4. The support directory is substantive.
5. HelpATL now has a meaningful policy/news layer instead of pure event dependency.

### Weak

1. Volunteer coverage is still dangerously concentrated in `Hands On Atlanta`, `Open Hand Atlanta`, and `Atlanta Community Food Bank`.
2. `Georgia Democracy Watch` is still thin.
3. `11` ongoing opportunities remain org-first instead of source-linked.
4. `Work, Money & Daily Life` is still the weakest support section.
5. HelpATL’s policy/news authority is credible, but still shallow for heavy policy users.

## 8) What to work on next

### Priority 1: Ongoing opportunity traceability

Why:

- this is the cleanest product-governance gap
- it improves trust without depending on unstable source volume

Targets:

- reduce source-null ongoing opportunities from `11` to `<= 4`
- focus first on `civic_engagement`, `immigrant_refugee`, and `housing`

### Priority 2: Policy-watch depth

Why:

- this is the highest-value gap for the “policy nerd” audience
- HelpATL now has the feed model to support it cleanly

Targets:

- add `1-2` more policy/explainer sources with strong public feeds
- likely next candidate: `GBPI`
- decide whether `Atlanta Civic Circle` should become a HelpATL-local policy source rather than only inherited
- grow `georgia-democracy-watch` from `1` next-30-day item to `5+`

### Priority 3: Support directory thin-lane expansion

Why:

- `Work, Money & Daily Life` is visibly the weakest support lane
- this is a contained expansion with low platform risk

Targets:

- grow `Work, Money & Daily Life` from `11` organizations to `20+`

### Priority 4: Volunteer concentration reduction

Why:

- still a real risk
- but the recent audits proved the next tier is weaker than expected

Targets:

- do not chase low-yield breadth sources as if they solve the problem
- only invest where sources can materially change the top-3 share
- treat `MedShare` and `Trees Atlanta` as the only currently proven diversification sources

## 9) Bottom line

HelpATL is now content-rich enough to feel like a real civic portal.

The next move should not be more indiscriminate source expansion. The highest-leverage work is:

1. fix ongoing opportunity traceability
2. deepen policy-watch authority
3. fill the weakest support lane

Those three moves strengthen trust and product identity more than squeezing marginal gains out of already-proven volunteer sources.
