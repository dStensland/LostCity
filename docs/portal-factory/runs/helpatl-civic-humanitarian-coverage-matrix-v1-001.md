# HelpATL Civic + Humanitarian Coverage Matrix V1 001

- Date: 2026-03-09
- Portal slug: `helpatl`
- Scope: concrete phased plan to make HelpATL the definitive Atlanta source for civic and humanitarian participation
- Decision: `go` for phased execution

## Objective

Make HelpATL the best Atlanta destination for:

1. `do something this week`
2. `commit to a cause`
3. `track public decisions`
4. `join civic participation, not just watch it`
5. `find trustworthy humanitarian support pathways`

This plan keeps the portal truthful by using the right data shape for each lane:

- `event`: dated public opportunities, meetings, trainings, action days
- `structured opportunity`: longer-term roles, orientations, recurring programs
- `directory/resource`: service infrastructure and support pathways

## Status Definitions

- `Strong`: users can reliably find meaningful inventory now
- `Adequate`: real coverage exists, but it is not yet balanced or deep
- `Thin`: a lane exists conceptually, but inventory or source depth is weak
- `Missing`: no meaningful user-facing coverage

## Phase Plan

## Phase 1: Harden The Core

- Objective: make current volunteer and government-watch coverage clearly excellent before adding new lanes.
- Why now: HelpATL already has enough breadth to matter. The risk is uneven quality, not missing product shape.

Work:

1. Improve yield and freshness for `united-way-atlanta`, `open-hand-atlanta`, and `habitat-for-humanity-atlanta`.
2. Audit duplicate pressure between aggregators and direct hosts.
3. Confirm each live section has strong visible inventory:
   - `Volunteer This Week`
   - `Ongoing Opportunities`
   - `Commit to a Cause`
   - `Government Meetings`
   - `School Board Watch`
4. Keep Hope, IRC, and similar role-driven orgs in `structured opportunity` until they expose trustworthy dated public calendars.

Exit criteria:

1. `50+` strong drop-in opportunities in a normal 14-day window.
2. `<10%` obvious duplicate pressure in top-of-feed volunteer results.
3. `80%+` of surfaced volunteer records have direct signup paths.
4. Every major current lane feels consumer-ready, not provisional.

## Phase 2: Civic Participation Layer

- Objective: move HelpATL from meeting watcher to participation hub.
- Why now: this is the biggest strategic gap between current state and “definitive source.”

Priority source owners:

1. `atlanta-npu-system`
2. `atlanta-municipal-clerk`
3. `league-of-women-voters-atlanta-fulton`
4. `center-for-civic-innovation`
5. `common-cause-georgia`
6. `canopy-atlanta-documenters`

Planned user-facing lanes:

1. `Neighborhood Participation`
2. `Boards & Commissions`
3. `Civic Training & Action`

Recommended shape by source:

| Source owner | Primary shape | Why |
|---|---|---|
| `atlanta-npu-system` | `event` + possible `directory/resource` | users need meeting dates plus neighborhood/NPU context |
| `atlanta-municipal-clerk` | `event` | public notices, hearings, deadlines, boards/commissions meetings |
| `league-of-women-voters-atlanta-fulton` | `event` + `structured opportunity` | trainings and recurring volunteer participation |
| `center-for-civic-innovation` | `event` | workshops, civic participation events, issue engagement |
| `common-cause-georgia` | `event` + `structured opportunity` | action days and recurring democracy-participation programs |
| `canopy-atlanta-documenters` | `structured opportunity` | recurring civic-documentation pathway rather than normal event feed |

Exit criteria:

1. HelpATL surfaces clear ways to participate in neighborhood and democratic process, not just observe it.
2. At least `4` high-trust civic participation source families are live.
3. Users can discover at least one real action path in each of:
   - neighborhood governance
   - boards/commissions
   - civic training/action
   - watchdog/documenters

## Phase 3: Humanitarian Breadth Completion

- Objective: turn humanitarian coverage from credible to balanced.
- Why now: HelpATL now covers many causes, but several are represented by one source family only.

Priority source owners:

1. `furkids`
2. one additional immigrant/refugee support source
3. one additional legal-aid or rights-oriented source
4. one additional housing/homelessness source
5. one additional health-oriented humanitarian source

Rules:

1. Use `event` only where the public source publishes dated opportunities.
2. Use `structured opportunity` where the source publishes role inventory but not reliable event dates.
3. Do not degrade trust by forcing non-event sources into the event feed.

Exit criteria:

1. No major humanitarian domain is represented by a single source family only.
2. Housing, immigrant/refugee, legal, medical, family support, and animal welfare all score at least `Adequate`.
3. Structured long-term opportunity inventory grows without muddying the drop-in feed.

## Phase 4: Support Resource Layer

- Objective: become definitive not just for “how to help,” but also “where help exists.”
- Why now: this is the step that changes HelpATL from action portal to civic utility.

Deliverables:

1. trusted service/resource directories by issue
2. issue pages for:
   - housing
   - immigration
   - education
   - transit
   - public safety
   - environment
3. stronger links between:
   - organizations
   - volunteer opportunities
   - meetings
   - trainings
   - support resources

Exit criteria:

1. A user can move from a cause or need to the relevant organizations, events, opportunities, and support resources.
2. HelpATL is no longer just an action feed; it becomes a trusted civic/humanitarian map.

## Phase 5: Authority Layer

- Objective: make HelpATL feel like the definitive Atlanta source, not just a broad directory.
- Why now: authority comes after coverage and trust are real.

Deliverables:

1. neighborhood landing pages
2. cause landing pages
3. editorial explainers
4. quality scorecards by lane
5. ongoing source gap monitoring

Exit criteria:

1. HelpATL is useful for both first-time visitors and repeat civic participants.
2. Coverage is legible by cause and neighborhood, not only by feed.
3. Operators can see which lanes are slipping before users do.

## Sequencing Rule

1. Do not expand deep into Phase 3 before Phase 2 exists. The bigger strategic gap is civic participation, not another marginal volunteer source.
2. Do not build Phase 4 directory/resource surfaces until the underlying issue coverage is credible enough to justify them.
3. Every phase should end with a run artifact under `docs/portal-factory/runs/` that records evidence, launch bar, and next moves.

## Product Test

If this plan succeeds, HelpATL should be able to answer all of these without feeling stretched:

1. `What can I do this week?`
2. `How do I commit to something bigger?`
3. `What public decisions are being made near me?`
4. `How do I actually participate in those processes?`
5. `Where do I go for help, not just volunteer work?`
