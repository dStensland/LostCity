A: Tight & Uniform

All cards same height/layout. Vertical data shows as 1-2 contextual chips (cuisine + price for dining, "exhibition opens today" for museum). Clean grid, easy to scan.

Pro: Scannable, consistent rhythm
Con: Wastes the rich data — a restaurant card and a trail card barely differ

B: Vertically Rich

Cards expand to show what matters for their type. Restaurants get ratings + cuisine + dining options. Museums get current exhibition inline. Trails get difficulty + length. Cards vary in height — the data earns its space.

Pro: Shows off the rich data, each card tells a complete story
Con: Variable heights, harder to scan, heavier page

C: Context-Adaptive

Cards render at two fidelity levels. In the unified stream: compact, scannable, key signals only. In a lane view ("See all Dining →"): expanded with full vertical data. Same component, different density.

Pro: Stream stays scannable, lanes go deep. Best of both worlds.
Con: Two render modes per card type = more engineering. But you're already building both.
