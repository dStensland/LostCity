A: Time-Based Groupings

Sections shift with the clock: "Open Right Now," "This Afternoon," "Tonight," "This Weekend." Each section mixes events + places. The label tells you when.

Legibility: High — "why is this here?" = "because it's relevant to your next few hours"
Risk: Morning/weekday views could feel thin

B: Vertical Lanes as Sections

Sections are the verticals: Arts & Culture, Dining, Nightlife, Outdoors, Entertainment. Within each, items sorted by temporal relevance. The label tells you what kind.

Legibility: High — "why is this grouped?" = "same category"
Risk: Feels like the current Places sub-tabs with extra steps

C: Hybrid — Time Lead, Vertical Lanes

Top section is time-driven ("Right Now" mixes everything relevant). Below that, vertical lanes take over — each with "See all →" into a focused lane view. Time answers "what now?" Verticals answer "what kind?"

Legibility: Highest — two clear organizing principles, both visible
Risk: More complex to build; need to decide when "Right Now" collapses (late night? nothing happening?)

Key context: All options support deep-linking into lanes. "See all →" on any vertical section opens the full filtered lane view. The question is about the default landing experience.
