# Atlanta / Hooky Activity Layer Phase 2

**Owner portal:** `atlanta`  
**Consumer portal:** `hooky`  
**Surface:** `consumer`  
**Status:** Defined / ready for one more major push  
**Last Updated:** 2026-03-11  
**Depends on:** `prds/atlanta-hooky-activity-layer-large-effort.md`, `prds/hooky-next-big-effort-workstream.md`, `prds/atlanta-activity-overlay-audit.md`, `prds/atlanta-activity-queue-a-feasibility.md`

---

## Purpose

Phase 1 proved the model:

- Atlanta can own reusable family activity intelligence
- Hooky can consume it through federation
- grouped overlay waves compound quickly

Phase 2 is the last large expansion push before the work changes character.

The goal is no longer "make the activity layer real."

The goal is:

- close the highest-value remaining coverage gaps
- improve metro spread
- improve seasonal fallback depth
- stop before the backlog turns into low-signal long-tail accumulation

---

## Current Baseline

Current baseline:

- `53` Atlanta-owned logical activity targets live
- `169` total live `venue_features` rows
- strongest covered lanes:
  - museums / cultural destinations
  - indoor play
  - arcades / mini golf / family fun centers
  - farms / animal destinations
  - seasonal fall-family anchors
  - water / summer day-trip destinations

That means the next push should not be "more of everything."

It should target the remaining gaps with the best payoff.

---

## Phase 2 Goal

Add the last meaningful wave of high-value family activity coverage before shifting from expansion mode to quality/packaging mode.

By the end of Phase 2, Hooky and Atlanta should be able to say:

1. the family activity layer is broad enough to feel intentionally complete in metro Atlanta
2. the remaining uncovered operators are edge cases, not obvious holes
3. the next problem is product presentation and ranking, not raw destination scarcity

---

## What Phase 2 Should Cover

Phase 2 should focus on four lanes.

## Lane A. Second-Wave Indoor Play / Energy Burn

These are the remaining all-weather fallback operators that still matter materially to family usefulness.

### Priority candidates

- additional `Sky Zone`-type locations if metro relevance is strong
- `Launch` / trampoline / jump operators if official surfaces are clean
- remaining credible family fun centers with official activity pages
- secondary indoor playground operators with strong neighborhood pull

### Why this lane matters

This is the most important fallback lane for:

- rainy days
- school-break gaps
- younger-kid energy-burn planning

---

## Lane B. Water / Splash / Summer Utility

The current layer has real summer utility now, but it can still be improved.

### Priority candidates

- additional destination-grade water parks or splash operators
- strong lake / tubing / paddling day-trip operators if family fit is obvious
- clean official splash/water destinations that are not just generic park pages

### Why this lane matters

This helps Hooky own:

- summer backup planning
- hot-weather family discovery
- school-break and visitor-day utility

---

## Lane C. Seasonal Farm / Fall / Holiday Depth

Phase 1 added real seasonal anchors. Phase 2 should make that layer feel metro-wide rather than isolated.

### Priority candidates

- more north-metro pumpkin / corn-maze farms with clean official pages
- one more south-metro or west-metro seasonal farm anchor
- holiday-light / tree-farm destinations with repeat annual value

### Why this lane matters

Competitors often feel fuller in fall and holiday periods because seasonal family destinations are highly visible.

This is one of the easiest ways to close perceived breadth gaps.

---

## Lane D. Adventure / Specialty Outing Gaps

This is the lane where Hooky can still look thin if it stops now.

### Priority candidates

- more ropes / aerial / climbing family destinations
- specialty animal or wildlife destinations
- distinctive mixed-age outing operators not covered by generic museums or arcades

### Why this lane matters

These are the “special outing” places that make a family portal feel richer than a directory of camps and museums.

---

## What Phase 2 Should Not Do

Avoid these traps:

1. do not add low-confidence venues just to hit round numbers
2. do not over-index on operators that only have weak third-party directory pages
3. do not add adult-tilted entertainment brands unless family fit is explicit
4. do not create duplicate Atlanta rows when a canonical venue already exists
5. do not let Phase 2 turn into open-ended long-tail cleanup

---

## Recommended Batch Structure

Phase 2 should run as `3` final grouped pushes.

## Batch P2-A. Indoor / Energy Burn Expansion

Focus:

- second-wave trampoline / jump / indoor-play operators
- secondary family fun centers with clean official surfaces

Exit gate:

- rainy-day and energy-burn coverage no longer has obvious metro holes

## Batch P2-B. Seasonal / Farm / Holiday Expansion

Focus:

- strong pumpkin patch / corn maze / tree farm / tulip / sunflower operators
- one or two best-in-class seasonal anchors by geography

Exit gate:

- fall and holiday family destination depth feels clearly stronger than it did at Phase 1 exit

## Batch P2-C. Water / Adventure / Specialty Expansion

Focus:

- remaining high-value water and adventure operators
- any last specialty destinations that create clear family-planning value

Exit gate:

- remaining uncovered operators are edge-case omissions, not obvious misses

---

## Ranking Rules For Phase 2 Candidates

Only promote candidates that score well on most of these:

1. strong official source surface
2. obvious family utility
3. broad enough appeal to matter to Hooky
4. meaningful metro/geographic coverage gain
5. useful seasonal or planning-moment value
6. low ambiguity about Atlanta ownership

If a candidate fails two or more of those, it should probably wait.

---

## Definition Of Done

Phase 2 is done when:

1. the obvious remaining family activity gaps are closed
2. the next backlog starts looking niche or repetitive
3. Atlanta-owned activity intelligence feels like a stable shared layer
4. Hooky’s remaining weakness is packaging, ranking, and UX presentation more than raw coverage

The current `53`-target baseline is close to that threshold already.

---

## After Phase 2

After this push, the workstream should shift from expansion to refinement:

- ranking and prioritization rules
- family-facing surfacing in Hooky
- lane scorecards
- confidence and freshness treatment
- product-level browse/feed packaging

That is a better use of time than chasing every remaining obscure operator indefinitely.
